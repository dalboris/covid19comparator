Coronavirus Comparator

This website is under construction.

It will allow you to compare the number of cases and/or deaths of COVID-19 (a.k.a. coronavirus) across countries.

It should be up and running hopefully by March 18, 2020.

If you are living in a country which already has more than 5 coronavirus deaths, please stay home. Go to worldometers.info/coronavirus to determine if this is your case. If not, please do not travel to any such country.

This is not "just a flu". This may be one of the deadliest pandemics of the last century. We're talking hundreds of thousands of deaths worldwide coming in the next few months, unless aggressive social distancing measures are taken in all countries infected by the virus (and more than 5 deaths most definitely means infected). Please read the official situation reports from the World Health Organization (WHO).

Fortunately, if you live in an infected country (which includes the United States), you can stop it. There is a simple antidote: stay home. It works. It's been proven. It isn't meant to save you: in fact, you will probably be fine no matter what you do. It is meant to save others. To save the 3.4% of people that die from the disease (official statement from WHO, March 3). Make the math: if 10% of your country's population catches the disease, and 3.4% of people die from it, that's about 1.1 million deaths in the United States alone. But this is only if people don't stay home.

Staying home means: don't go to school, don't go to work, don't go to religious celebrations, don't go to bars/restaurants/etc, don't hang out in your local park (yes, you can take a 5-min walk, including with your dog, but no longer and stay 6-feet away from other people), don't go visit your family, even for just a dinner, and even your close family (parents/siblings/children), don't go visit your friends, and most importantly, don't travel for even one mile/kilometer. Just watch TV or your favorite streaming service instead. The only reason you should ever leave your front door is to buy food or medicine, to make a daily 5-min walk not to become insane, or if you are seriously ill and need to see a doctor.

Obviously, there are exceptions. Medical staff should quite obviously go to work. Staff part of the food supply chain should quite obviously go to work. Other essential administrative jobs for the country not to fall apart should quite obviously go to work. Otherwise, just stay home.
